Drowning This Hatred

Once, there was a girl.

Whose daughter she was is not important.

One day, she went to play by the sea and was drowned.

After her death, she became a bird. Every day, she carried stones and twigs to the sea, trying to fill it.

In the original story, no reason is given for her actions. People have therefore imagined many possibilities: perhaps she overestimated her own strength; perhaps she did not want another girl to be drowned; perhaps she was simply filled with anger.

The sea appears endless. How could a small bird ever fill it?

When facing a power that seems impossible to challenge, individual strength can feel insignificant. Yet kindness has the right to exist. Anger has the right to exist.

To throw a stone into an endless sea is an act of resistance.

And perhaps, one of the most powerful things a person can do.
